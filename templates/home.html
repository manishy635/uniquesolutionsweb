Technologies Used

Total Surveys Station

The total station is mounted on a tripod and leveled before use. The other part of a total station, the electronic distance measuring device, measures the distance from the instrument to its target. The total station allows measurement of this type to be taken by simply targeting someone as they move down the avalanche chute (assuming it's safe to do so).

Leveling Station

Surveyor's level, instrument used in surveying to measure the height of distant points in relation to a bench mark (a point for which the height above sea level is accurately known). It consists of a telescope fitted with a spirit level and, generally, mounted on a tripod. It is used in conjunction with a graduated rod placed at the point to be measured and sighted through the telescope.

Global Positioning System

The Global Positioning System (GPS) is a satellite-based navigation system made up of a network of 24 satellites. GPS satellites circle the earth twice a day in a very precise orbit and transmit signal information to earth.
